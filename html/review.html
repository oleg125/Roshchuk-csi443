Louis Vuitton

Not long ago I had a conversation with a friend about which brand of clothing is the most expensive. This gave me the idea to see if the websites that support these brands are of good quality also. I came upon a brand called Louis Vuitton which the link to can be found here

Top

Navigation and Structure

At first look, this website looks professional and well made. The colors are based on their product and the website design matches. The navigation bar is responsive and upon changing to a smaller screen, the navigation bar becomes a hamburger menu. The Navigation bar is simple and includes the things that are important. The navigation bar also includes a search bar for faster navigation. Three out of the five links are dropdown which gives choices for what the user needs in the specific category. In general, it is easy to use.
Top

Design

The responsive design works like most designs. When using on a mobile device, the website shrinks, and the navigation bar becomes a hamburger menu, like usual. The color all matches the content and the design is well thought of. However, I did find a flaw in the shrinking. When you shrink the website and then make it full screen again, the letters overlap the first image. This happens when you shrink to fast, and it isn’t visible if you are using it on a mobile device which is good. There are no ads and all their images are of their products. There are no distractions and looking at the website it is easy to see what they want you to see.
Top

Content

Looking through their products, the grid to display the products is well made. Also, the zoom system to check the products in more detail works well. Most of their products are match the theme which is of red, gold, and black (Leather). Also, it is good to see that they made the navigation bar a neutral color because when going to view the products there are no colors that should stick out. In other words, when going to the shoes page for men, the color theme makes it easier to view the products that there are no distractions. However, this website also includes old information. Below is a picture of it, the reason that I can’t do a list is that there is a flaw which I will talk about later.
Top

Interactivity

Looking at the interactivity, specifically the search bar, I typed in “sneaker” (Links to an external site.)Links to an external site. to see what I would be able to find. The first thing that I saw was products which consisted of a sneaker in their name. Also, there was a tab which gave me the ability to view three stores, 0 services, and 4 LV NOW. In the top right-hand corner, there was a dropdown which gave me the ability to click on MyLV which showed login. This was hard to find, but I managed. In that dropdown, there is a create my LV account and a sign in. The web site allows doing what you need to. In other words, if you need to sign in and buy something, it gives you the ability to do that.
Top

Suggested Improvements

Looking through the website, I saw that it was well made and easy to use. The biggest flaw in this website is that sometimes you open it, the website shows a “Optimize your Experience” page. In other words, it wants me to update my browser, even though I have the latest one. This is the reason that you will not be able to share links. If you made a copy of the link and sent it to someone, they would not be able to open that link, instead, it will take it to the Update browser and then homepage. Since this store includes very expensive products, I would add a Video Effect with CSS Blend Modes to be able to view the product 360 degrees. One more thing that I would recommend it with the dropdown for login and new user. I don’t like how it is hard to find. Also, the right-hand-corner looks a little messy, so I would put some spacing in between the links. In general, I was expecting this website to be much better than what I discovered it to be.
